# CodePrep Design

## Thesis

CodePrep should feel like a quiet technical interview desk: one question, one committed decision, one useful explanation. It is practice software, not a gamified course dashboard.

## Primary Flow

1. Read a challenge immediately.
2. Choose the difficulty for the next question.
3. Commit to one answer.
4. Review the tested concept and technical rationale.
5. Move to the next challenge or revisit the session record.

## Signature Interaction

The explanation remains visually present but intentionally unavailable until an answer is selected. That spatial continuity makes the reveal feel like feedback on a decision rather than a separate results page.

## Constraints

- Demo mode must begin with a real challenge, not a marketing or empty screen.
- Keep answer choices scannable and keyboard friendly.
- Prefer technical vocabulary and plain feedback over points, streaks, or invented rankings.
- Motion is limited to state entry and direct press feedback.
- Mobile preserves the order question, choices, rationale.
